772  /* This function performs a post-analysis of blocked geometries (see analyseBlockedGeometry for
773  * more detail on the pre-analysis).
774  *
775  * Basically identifies which parameters shall be fixed to make geometries having blocking
776  * constraints fixed, while not leading to redundant/conflicting constraints. These parameters
777  * must belong to blocked geometry. This is, groups may comprise parameters belonging to blocked
778  * geometry and parameters belonging to unconstrained geometry. It is licit that the latter
779  * remain as dependent parameters. The former are referred to as "blockable parameters".
780  *
781  * Extending this concept, there may be unsatisfiable groups (because they do not comprise any
782  * bloackable parameter), and it is the desired outcome NOT to satisfy such groups.
783  *
784  * There is not a single combination of fixed parameters from the blockable parameters that
785  * satisfy all the dependency groups. However:
786  *
787  * 1) some combinations do not satisfy all the dependency groups that must be satisfied (e.g.
788  * fixing one group containing two blockable parameters with a given one may result in another
789  * group, fixable only by the former, not to be satisfied). This leads, in a subsequent
790  * diagnosis, to satisfiable unsatisfied groups.
791  *
792  * 2) some combinations lead to partially redundant constraints, that the solver will silently
793  * drop in a subsequent diagnosis, thereby reducing the rank of the system fixing less than it
794  * should.
795  *
796  * Implementation rationale (at this time):
797  *
798  * The implementation is on the order of the groups provided by the QR decomposition used to
799  * reveal the parameters (see System::identifyDependentParameters in GCS). Zeros are made over
800  * the pilot of the full R matrix of the QR decomposition, which is a top triangular
801  * matrix.This, together with the permutation matrix, allow one to know groups of dependent
802  * parameters (cols between rank and full size). Each group refers to a new parameter not
803  * affected by the rank in combination with other free parameters intervening in the rank
804  * (because of the triangular shape of the R matrix). This results in that each the first column
805  * between the rank and the full size, may only depend on a number of parameters, while the last
806  * full size column may be dependent on any amount of previously introduced parameters.
807  *
808  * Thus the rationale is: start from the last group (having **potentially** the larger amount of
809  * parameters) and selecting as blocking for that group the latest blockable parameter. Because
810  * previous groups do not have access to the last parameter, this can never interfere with
811  * previous groups. However, because the last parameter may not be a blockable one, there is a
812  * risk of selecting a parameter common with other group, albeit the probability is reduced and
813  * probably (I have not demonstrated it though and I am not sure), it leads to the right
814  * solution in one iteration.
815  *
816  */
817  bool analyseBlockedConstraintDependentParameters(std::vector<int>& blockedGeoIds,
818  std::vector<double*>& params_to_block) const;
819
